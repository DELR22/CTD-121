Section 01

Introduction & Student Learning Outcomes

Technical drawing is a language, and like any language, it depends on legibility to communicate. In architectural and interior design practice, hand-lettered title blocks are not decorative. They are the identification system of a drawing set — they record what is drawn, at what scale, by whom, and for what purpose.

This assignment extends the work begun in Assignment 1 by completing each of the four foam board formats with a hand-lettered title block. Students will use the same technical instruments to construct precise lettering guides within which all text will be written freehand.

Recording the making process is as important as the final result. Students are expected to photograph or scan their work at each meaningful stage of progress and upload these records to the course’s collaborative board. In design practice, process documentation is evidence of thinking, decision-making, and project development. Learning to record work in progress is itself a professional skill.

The title block for each 11″ × 8.5″ board will identify the drawing’s specific content. The 11″ × 17″ board will include both a title block and a Definition of Scale, which contextualize the proportional relationships explored in Part 2 of Assignment 1. In all cases, the example templates provided serve as the reference for measurements, layout, and required information fields.

Bloom’s Revised Taxonomy (2001)

  • Remember The components and conventions of a professional title block as used in architectural and interior design technical drawing.
  • Understand The function of lettering guides as a structural framework that ensures consistent letter height, proportion, and baseline alignment across a drawing.
  • Apply Freehand technical lettering within instrument-drawn guides to produce clear, consistent, and legible text across all four foam board formats.
  • Analyze How the size, placement, and content of a title block contribute to the overall organization and legibility of a technical drawing.
  • Evaluate The quality of completed title blocks against standards of letter consistency, line weight, and spatial organization.
  • Create A visual record of the making process by documenting progressive stages of work through photographs or scans, organized and shared on the course collaborative board.

CIDA / NASAD Framework

  • Demonstrate Foundational graphic communication proficiency by constructing and completing instrument-guided, hand-lettered title blocks in accordance with professional drawing conventions.
  • Produce Technical drawings that meet professional graphic standards by integrating legible, proportionally consistent freehand lettering into completed drawing formats.
  • Articulate Drawing identification conventions by accurately recording drawing-specific information within each title block field.
  • Demonstrate Awareness of the relationship between drawing organization and design communication, recognizing the title block and Definition of Scale as instruments for contextualizing and identifying technical work within a professional drawing set.
  • Document And communicate the design process by photographing or scanning progressive stages of work and contributing them to the shared environment, reflecting professional standards for process transparency and collaborative critique.

Section 02 Materials & Instructions

General Instructions

  1. Download and review the example templates before beginning. The templates serve as a reference for all title block measurements, layout proportions, field locations, and required text content. Dimensions and field specifications are defined by the templates.
  2. All lettering guides must be constructed using drawing instruments. No freehand measuring or estimation is permitted.
  3. All text within the title blocks must be lettered freehand using the 2mm lead holder, within the instrument-drawn guides.
  4. Lettering guides must be drawn using the 0.5mm mechanical pencil, consistent with the structural line convention established in Assignment 1.
  5. Do not begin lettering until all guides for a given title block have been fully constructed and verified against the template.
  6. Photograph or scan your work at each meaningful stage of progress, including after guide construction and after lettering is complete on each board. Upload all process images to your designated space on the course collaborative board as work develops. Process documentation uploaded after the fact or only at submission will not meet this requirement.

Section 03

Drawing Formats

Each foam board completed in Assignment 1 becomes a drawing format for this assignment. You will hand-letter a title block directly onto each board following the specifications described below. Download the full-size PDF examples from Blackboard to use as reference while you work.

Part 1 Formats — Three (3) 11″ × 8.5″ Boards · Scale 1/2″ = 1′-0″

ID 101

Triangle Pattern

Landscape board, 11″ × 8.5″. A repeating pinwheel of right triangles fills the drawing area at scale 1/2″ = 1′-0″. The title block occupies the lower strip and includes the course name, student name, scale, and sheet number 1/4.

ID 102

Circle Pattern

Landscape board, 11″ × 8.5″. A repeating overlapping-circle (petal) pattern fills the drawing area at the same scale. Title block includes course name, student name, scale, and sheet number 2/4.

ID 103

Rhomboid Pattern

Landscape board, 11″ × 8.5″. A repeating interlocking rhomboid pattern fills the drawing area at the same scale. Title block includes course name, student name, scale, and sheet number 3/4.

Part 2 Format — One (1) 11″ × 17″ Board · Scales 1/2″, 1/4″, 1/8″

ID 104

Scaling Format

Portrait board, 11″ × 17″. The board is divided into three vertical columns, each rendering the same pattern at a progressively smaller scale: 1/2″ = 1′-0″ (left), 1/4″ = 1′-0″ (center), and 1/8″ = 1′-0″ (right). A fourth panel contains a hand-lettered Definition of Scale paragraph. The title block reads Scale: As Indicated and sheet number 4/4.

Section 04

Evaluation Rubric

The assignment is evaluated across three criteria totaling 100 points. Lettering Guide Construction and Lettering Quality carry equal weight as the core technical requirements. Process Documentation reflects the professional expectation of recording work progressively throughout the assignment.

Criterion Excellent Proficient Developing
1. Lettering Guide Construction & Measurement Accuracy 40 pts total
21 – 40 pts
All lettering guides are constructed using drawing instruments at the exact measurements specified in the templates. Guide lines are straight, consistently spaced, and correctly positioned within the title block area on all four boards. The Definition of Scale guide structure on the 11″ × 17″ board is complete and accurate.
11 – 20 pts
Guide construction is mostly accurate. Minor measurement deviations or inconsistencies in spacing are present on one board but do not significantly affect the overall structure. All required guide areas are attempted.
0 – 10 pts
Guide construction is inaccurate or incomplete on multiple boards. Measurements do not correspond to the template, guides appear to be estimated rather than instrument-drawn, or one or more title block areas are missing their guide structure entirely.
2. Lettering Quality & Title Block Completion 40 pts total
21 – 40 pts
All four title blocks are fully completed with the correct information in each field. Lettering is upright, consistent in height, and evenly spaced within the guides across all boards. The Definition of Scale on the 11″ × 17″ board is complete and legible. The 2mm lead holder produces crisp, dark, clean text throughout.
11 – 20 pts
All or nearly all title blocks are completed. Lettering is generally consistent but shows minor variation in height, spacing, or weight in one or two fields. The overall submission is legible and demonstrates a full attempt.
0 – 10 pts
One or more title blocks are incomplete or missing required information fields. Lettering is inconsistent in height or spacing across multiple boards, or text is too light, irregular, or illegible to read clearly.
3. Process Documentation 20 pts total
15 – 20 pts
Process images are uploaded to the course collaborative board progressively throughout the assignment. At minimum, photographs or scans after guide construction and after lettering completion are present for all four boards. Images are clear, well-lit, and taken straight-on.
9 – 14 pts
Most required process stages are documented. One board may be missing a stage image, or one or two images are poorly lit or angled but still legible as documentation.
0 – 8 pts
Process documentation is incomplete, uploaded only at submission, or missing for multiple boards. Images do not adequately demonstrate progressive stages of work.
Total 100 pts 54 pts max 28 pts max
